<h3>... How do I work this?</h3>

That's where I found myself in February of 2018 with the [volatility markets](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-02-06/the-day-the-vix-doubled-tales-of-volmageddon) upside down for a few hours.  I was more than out of a job, I was out of the last two years of my life that I  had spent building a business, my business.

Prior to that, I had spent most of my professional career in the banking and brokerage industry.   Before that, I was a jarhead with a couple of combat tours in Iraq under my belt.  If we go way-way back, I was once a kid from Grand Rapids, MI that liked to play sports... sometimes.  I also liked to pay attention in school... slightly less than sometimes. 

The process to unwind all of the broken and formerly moving parts of my business was a drawn out and traumatic process that allowed me ample time for me to reflect on what was wrong with my life and all the things I did to cause it to be this way.  

<h3>And you may ask yourself... "Where the (*&@ do I go from here?"</h3>

Enter:

<a href="https://www.codeplatoon.org"><img src="/static/code-platoon-logo-black.png"></a>

[Code Platoon](https://www.codeplatoon.org) is a non-profit located in downtown Chicago, IL that helps veterans and spouses transition into careers in tech.  It was an intense 13-week boot camp style program that gave me the basics of programming.  We worked on a tech stack that included JavaScript (React) and Python (Django).  Immediately after graduation, we rolled into a week of interviews with sponsor companies that were offering paid internships and full-time positions.

I connected with 8th Light, a computer software consultancy that spends significant time and resources developing an apprentice into a fulling functioning (and billable) software crafter.

Feel free to reach out to me with any questions about 8th Light, Code Platoon, or the trials and tribulations of running a hedge fund.
